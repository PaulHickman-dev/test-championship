Overview

This tool explores the points system used in the ICC Test Championship.

In the same way that if you toss a coin millions of times, you would expect to get heads 50% of the time and tails 50%, by simulating what happens when you play the championship repeatedly with random results, it reveals the chances each team has of winning and exposes any bias in the rules.

As the purpose of this tool is to determine the fairness of the rules, not decide which country is best at cricket, the simulation assumes that all teams are equally skillful - i.e. in any match, both teams have an equal chance of winning.

A false assumption in the real world, I'll grant you, but if it were true, and the rules of the Test Championship were fair, all teams should have an equal chance to qualify for the final.

Spoiler alert: they don't!
